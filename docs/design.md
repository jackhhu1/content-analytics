# Design: Niche Viral Intelligence Feed for Content Creators

## Problem Statement

Content creators at 1K–50K followers can't systematically identify and learn from viral
content in their niche. The founder discovered that modeling off viral posts dramatically
outperforms original ideas — but finding that content requires 45+ minutes of manual
scrolling, and when you do save something useful, it disappears into a DM thread with
no structure, no analysis, and no way to act on it.

The product replaces two costly behaviors:
1. Manual scrolling to discover what's working in your niche
2. Saving content to DMs with no extraction layer

## Demand Evidence

Personal validation: founder is a creator who empirically tested "model off viral posts"
vs "original ideas" and found viral modeling consistently outperforms. The workaround
(sending reels to self via DMs) is an active behavior, not a hypothetical — it already
exists, it just produces zero usable output.

No paying customers yet. Single-user validation only at this stage.

## Status Quo

Current workflow:
1. Scroll Instagram/YouTube manually (~45min+)
2. Find a reel that performed well in adjacent niche
3. Forward it to own DMs to "save" it
4. Eventually lose track of it or forget why it was interesting
5. No extraction — no hook breakdown, no format notes, no template

What this costs: time (scrolling), quality (no system means no compounding), and
consistency (can't replicate what you don't remember why worked).

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

**Target user:** Solo content creator, 1K–50K followers, Instagram/Reels-first, building
an audience pre-monetization. Not yet at the scale where their own analytics tell them
enough — they need external signal.

**Narrowest wedge:** Give this one person a weekly curated feed of the top 10-20 viral
posts from 3-5 accounts they already respect in their niche — each one pre-analyzed —
so they can pick one to recreate with their own angle without scrolling.

This is the smallest version someone would use daily. It removes both the scrolling tax
and the "what do I do with this?" problem in one shot.

## Constraints

- Pre-product: no users, no code, brand new repo
- Solo builder (side project with startup potential)
- Instagram scraping is reliability risk (rate limits, API access, ToS)
- Analysis quality depends entirely on what structured data is extractable from reels
  (transcript, caption, thumbnail — video frames optional)

## Premises

1. Core value = structural pattern extraction from viral content (hook type, format,
   topic, CTA) — not just flagging that something went viral. Pacing is aspirational
   for v2 but requires video analysis; not a v1 premise.

2. **Revised:** Primary validation signal = viral reels already exist on this topic
   (format + topic validation in one). YouTube search is a secondary layer, not core.
   Rationale: Instagram is interest-graph-pushed, not search-pull. The better question
   is "does this format + topic already work in my niche?" not "does anyone search this
   on YouTube?"

3. Creators at 1K–50K have too little personal data to self-optimize — their 50-200
   posts aren't statistically significant. External viral signals are the right input.

4. Immediate product = a niche discovery feed (not a passive swipe file). The user
   shouldn't have to find the content — it should come to them pre-analyzed.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: "URL → Card" Passive Swipe File
Paste a Reel URL, get a structured template card back. Build a library of saved cards.
Effort S/M. Rejected because it still requires the user to find the content manually —
only solves half the problem.

### Approach B: Full Niche Intelligence Dashboard
Automated feed of trending reels in your niche, content map, pattern library that
grows automatically. Effort L/XL. The 10x version — but too many assumptions to
validate at once.

### Approach C: "Forward to Bot" (Telegram / iOS Shortcut)
Zero behavior change — forward reels to a bot, get analysis back. Effort S. Considered
as the 48-hour prototype path, but chat interface limits the library/discovery features
that turn out to be core.

## Recommended Approach

**"Niche Viral Intelligence Feed" — scoped MVP**

1. **Niche setup:** User inputs 3-5 Instagram accounts they consider top performers in
   their niche (or hashtags). One-time setup.

2. **Scraping + ranking:** Weekly on Monday 6am UTC (configurable via `SCRAPE_CRON_SCHEDULE`
   env var). Apify Instagram actor fetches recent posts from each niche account.
   
   Composite engagement score (weighted, reflecting Instagram's current algorithm):
   `score = views + (likes * 3) + (comments * 5) + (shares * 10)`
   All terms are 0 when unavailable (likes may be hidden in some regions; saves/shares
   may not be returned by Apify). Score is computed on whatever signals are available.
   
   Overperformer = score >= 1.5x median(score of last 30 posts for that account).
   Tie-breaking: higher absolute share count wins (if equal, higher comments).
   Top 5 overperformers per account are surfaced (25 max for 5 accounts per week).
   
   When Apify run completes, it fires a webhook to `/api/webhook/apify`. Handler verifies
   `APIFY_WEBHOOK_SECRET`, inserts posts, and enqueues for extraction. Idempotent:
   INSERT uses ON CONFLICT (account_id, instagram_post_id) DO NOTHING.

3. **AI extraction:** For each overperforming post, GPT-4o extracts from caption +
   thumbnail (video frame analysis is out of scope for v1):
   - Hook type (curiosity gap / shock / contrast / story / question)
   - Format (talking head / B-roll / text overlay / mixed)
   - Topic/angle (1-sentence description)
   - CTA type (follow / comment / link / save)
   - Pacing: omitted from v1 — requires video frame sampling, not available from
     caption/thumbnail alone. Added in v2 if audio transcription is integrated.

   **Extraction ordering (decoupled from storage):**
   1. Call GPT-4o immediately using the live Apify CDN URL (GPT-4o accepts image URLs)
   2. After extraction succeeds: download thumbnail to Supabase Storage as a best-effort
      async step (for permanent display — Instagram CDN URLs expire)
   3. If storage download fails: extraction already succeeded, thumbnail_storage_path=null,
      feed shows placeholder image only
   
   This decoupling removes the failure mode where a storage failure blocks extraction.

4. **Feed UI:** Clean grid of analyzed posts, newest first. Each card shows stored
   thumbnail + extracted structure. Click to expand → "Recreate with your twist"
   template with these fields:
   - Hook sentence: [write your version of the hook]
   - Format: [talking head / B-roll / text overlay]
   - Topic angle: [your unique take on the topic]
   - CTA: [what you want viewers to do]
   Empty state: "Your niche feed is being built — check back Monday." Error state per
   account: "Couldn't fetch @accountname this week — will retry next cycle."

5. **Save + playbook:** User can save cards to their personal playbook. Unsave removes
   from playbook (card remains in main feed). Accessible via `/playbook` route — same
   grid layout as main feed, filtered to saved cards. No save limit in v1.
   Data model: `saved_cards(id, user_id FK, post_id FK, saved_at)`.

**Stack:** Next.js + Supabase + Apify (Instagram scraping) + OpenAI API
**Auth:** Magic link email. Session TTL: 7 days. User-specific niche accounts stored in
`niche_accounts(id, user_id FK, instagram_handle, created_at)` — Supabase RLS policy
restricts reads/writes to `auth.uid() = user_id`. Each user sees only their own feed.
**Background jobs:** Supabase Edge Functions have CPU time limits (~2s wall-clock on free
tier, 60s on Pro). Processing 10-20 posts per run (Apify fetch + thumbnail download +
GPT-4o call) will exceed free-tier limits. Architecture: a single cron Edge Function
enqueues post IDs into a `scrape_queue` table; a second Edge Function processes one
post per invocation (triggered via pg_cron every 30s). This fan-out pattern keeps each
invocation well within limits. Alternative: small VPS (Railway/Fly.io $5/mo) running a
simple Node.js cron if Supabase queue complexity is unwanted overhead for v1.
**Hosting:** Vercel (frontend, Next.js only) + Supabase (database, storage, edge
functions — free tier to start)

**Core data model (v1 tables):**
- `users` — managed by Supabase Auth
- `niche_accounts(id, user_id FK, instagram_handle, created_at)` — RLS: user_id = auth.uid()
- `scrape_runs(id, account_id FK, user_id FK, apify_run_id TEXT, status, triggered_at, completed_at)` — tracks Apify runs; status: 'running'|'completed'|'failed'
- `posts(id, account_id FK, user_id FK, instagram_post_id, instagram_cdn_url, caption, thumbnail_storage_path, view_count, like_count, comment_count, share_count, composite_score, account_baseline_score, is_overperformer, scraped_at, hook_type, format_type, topic_angle, cta_type, extraction_status)` — RLS: user_id = auth.uid() [direct column, not join]. UNIQUE(account_id, instagram_post_id).
- `saved_cards(id, user_id FK, post_id FK, saved_at, user_notes JSONB)` — RLS: user_id = auth.uid(). user_notes stores editable "Recreate with your twist" fields: {hook_sentence, format_choice, topic_angle, cta}.
- `scrape_queue(id, account_id FK, user_id FK, post_id FK, status, created_at, processed_at, processing_started_at)` — status: 'pending'|'processing'|'completed'|'failed'. Stuck recovery: processing_started_at < now()-5min → reset to pending.
- `scrape_errors(id, account_id FK, user_id FK, error_message, failed_at)` — for scraping failure monitoring (see TODOS)

**Follower count:** fetched fresh at each scrape time and stored on the `posts` row.
Not cached across scrapes (follower count drift matters for engagement rate accuracy).

**Edge cases:**
- Account with <30 posts: use all available posts as baseline
- Tie-breaking at overperformer boundary: sort by absolute likes (higher wins)
- Thumbnail download failure: extraction proceeds without thumbnail, hook_type marked as "text-only-extraction", thumbnail_storage_path left null
- Session expiry: redirect to `/login` on 401, silent re-auth not implemented in v1

**Skip in v1:** YouTube integration, your own content analytics, scheduling/posting,
mobile app, team features, notifications.

## Reviewer Concerns (unresolved after 2 review iterations)

These are real engineering questions an implementer will need to answer. Not blocking
the design, but should be resolved before writing production code:

- Feed "newest first" is ambiguous — define as `scraped_at` (most recent scrape wins),
  not post publish date, since publish date requires an additional API field
- "Recreate with your twist" template fields: should be editable text inputs saved to a
  `user_notes` column on `saved_cards`, not static display labels
- `/playbook` unsave behavior: immediate (optimistic UI update), not deferred to next load
- Supabase Storage egress: thumbnails should be served through Vercel's CDN (store URL
  as a signed Supabase URL with long TTL, or proxy through Next.js `/api/thumb` route)
  to avoid Supabase egress costs once traffic grows
- OpenAI rate limiting: add exponential backoff in the queue processor; log failures to
  `posts.extraction_status = 'failed'` so they can be retried

## Open Questions

- Scraping reliability: Instagram actively rate-limits and blocks scrapers. Apify's actor
  is the best off-the-shelf solution but has failure modes. Need a fallback strategy
  (manual URL input as Approach A if automated scraping fails for a given account).
- Extraction accuracy: How good is GPT-4o at extracting hook type from caption +
  thumbnail alone (without audio transcript)? Need to test with ~20 real reels before
  trusting the extraction layer.
- Pricing: What do creators 1K–50K actually pay for tools? VidIQ free tier is their
  reference point. $10-20/month is probably the ceiling for early adopters. Virlo is
  at $49/month which may be aspirational ceiling.
- Niche definition: "3-5 accounts you respect" is easy to understand but may produce
  narrow results. Consider allowing hashtag-based discovery as an alternative input.
- YouTube integration: explicitly out of scope for v1 and v2 until the Instagram
  viral-reels signal proves insufficient for topic validation. Do not add YouTube
  features until at least 3 users explicitly request it after using the product.

## Success Criteria

- 5 real creators (other than founder) use the feed daily for 2 weeks
- At least 3 of them say "I've made a reel based on something I saw in this feed"
- At least 1 says "I wouldn't go back to manually scrolling"
- Extraction accuracy: creator agrees the hook type/format is correct >80% of the time

## Distribution Plan

**v1:** Direct outreach. Founder shares with other creators in their network. DM
distribution in creator communities (Twitter/X, Telegram groups). Recruit beta users
explicitly from the 1K–50K follower band — not mega-influencers who have teams, not
nano-creators who aren't yet consistent enough to benefit.

**Word-of-mouth:** If output quality is high, creators will naturally share the tool
when asked "how do you find what to post?" No in-product attribution feature in v1 —
watch for organic mentions first before building referral infrastructure.

**No app store:** Web app only. Lower friction than native app, no review process.

**CI/CD:** Vercel auto-deploy on push to main. No special release process for v1.

## Dependencies

- Apify account (Instagram scraping actor) — ~$0.002/reel
- OpenAI API key (GPT-4o extraction)
- Vercel + Supabase free tiers to start
- Instagram ToS risk: scraping public posts is a gray area. Mitigate by only scraping
  public accounts the user explicitly inputs (not mass-scraping).

## The Assignment

**Before writing a single line of code:** Find 3 other creators you know personally and
show them the following: a manually-created version of one "analyzed card" for a viral
reel in their niche. Just a screenshot or doc. Ask: "If you woke up every Monday and
had 10 of these waiting for you — what would you do differently?" If all 3 say "I'd use
this immediately" — start building. If they ask "what IS this?" — you need to sharpen
the pitch before building.
